# Damage Severity

To determine the approximate severity of damage taken by a character, we can use the following table based on the character's level and the amount of damage received:

| Character Level | Nuisance   | Deadly     |
|-----------------|------------|------------|
| 1-4             | 5 (1d10)   | 11 (2d10)  |
| 5-10            | 11 (2d10)  | 22 (4d10)  |
| 11-16           | 22 (4d10)  | 55 (10d10) |
| 17-20           | 55 (10d10) | 99 (18d10) |

**Nuisance**: Nuisance damage rarely poses a risk of death to characters of the levels shown, but a severely weakened character might be laid low by this damage.

**Deadly**: Deadly damage poses a significant threat to characters of the levels shown and could potentially kill such a character that's missing many Hit Points.

# Improvising Damage

When improvising damage, the following table can be used to help determine the approximate damage for the effect. These are just examples. You can use them to determine the damage of other effects that are similar in nature.

| Damage | Examples                                                                                                 |
|--------|----------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------|
| 1d10   | Burned by coals, hit by a falling bookcase, pricked by a poison needle                                   |
| 2d10   | Struck by lightning, stumbling into a firepit                                                            |
| 4d10   | Hit by falling rubble in a collapsing tunnel, tumbling into a vat of acid                                |
| 10d10  | Crushed by compacting walls, hit by whirling steel blades, wading through lava                           |
| 18d10  | Submerged in lava, hit by a crashing flying fortress                                                     |
| 24d10  | Tumbling into a vortex of fire on the Elemental Plane of Fire, crushed in the jaws of a godlike creature |
